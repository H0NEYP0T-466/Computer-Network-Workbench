## 🌟 Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## ✅ Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- 🤝 **Being respectful and inclusive** in language and actions
- 🎯 **Focusing on constructive feedback** and criticism
- 🙏 **Showing empathy** towards others
- 🤲 **Offering help** to those who need it
- 🎉 **Celebrating achievements** and contributions
- 📚 **Sharing knowledge** and learning from others
- 💡 **Being open to new ideas** and perspectives
- 🙋 **Taking responsibility** for our actions and mistakes
- 🎓 **Supporting fellow students** in their learning journey

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- 🚫 **Harassment**: Offensive comments, intimidation, or threats
- 🚫 **Discrimination**: Based on personal characteristics
- 🚫 **Trolling**: Deliberately inflammatory comments
- 🚫 **Spam**: Unsolicited advertising or promotion
- 🚫 **Doxxing**: Publishing private information without consent
- 🚫 **Sexual content**: Inappropriate or unwelcome
- 🚫 **Violence**: Threats or encouragement of violence
- 🚫 **Sabotage**: Deliberately undermining others' work
- 🚫 **Academic dishonesty**: Plagiarism or cheating

## 👮 Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- 📋 Clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior
- ⚖️ Taking appropriate and fair corrective action
- 🗑️ Removing, editing, or rejecting inappropriate content
- 🚫 Temporarily or permanently banning contributors
- 📢 Communicating reasons for moderation decisions

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

- 📁 **GitHub Repository**: Issues, pull requests, discussions

- 📅 **Events**: Project-related meetings or gatherings

## ⚖️ Enforcement

### Enforcement Actions

Maintainers may take the following actions:

1. **Warning**: Verbal or written notice
2. **Temporary Ban**: Temporary removal from project spaces
3. **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from project
4. **Content Removal**: Deleting inappropriate content
5. **Pull Request Rejection**: Rejecting contributions that violate standards

### Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these guidelines:

- 🎯 **Proportionate**: Actions proportional to the violation
- ⚖️ **Fair**: Consistent treatment for all community members
- 📝 **Documented**: Clear records of violations and actions
- 🔒 **Confidential**: Privacy respected when appropriate

## 📢 Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Contact Project Maintainers**
   - Open a confidential issue: [Report Abuse](https://github.com/H0NEYP0T-466/Computer-Network-Workbench/issues)
   - Use the title format: `[COC] Brief description`

2. **What to Include**
   - Your contact information
   - Description of the incident
   - When and where it occurred
   - Any witnesses
   - Supporting evidence (screenshots, links)
   - Desired outcome

### What Happens Next

1. **Acknowledgment**: Within 48 hours
2. **Investigation**: Review of the incident
3. **Resolution**: Appropriate action taken
4. **Follow-up**: Reporter informed of outcome

### Confidentiality

- Reports are treated confidentially
- Reporter identity protected when possible
- Only necessary parties informed

### No Retaliation

- Retaliation against reporters is prohibited
- Good faith reports are protected
- False reports may result in action

## 🎓 Community Guidelines

### For Contributors

- ✅ Follow the project's naming conventions
- ✅ Test configurations in Packet Tracer before submitting
- ✅ Document your work clearly
- ✅ Be respectful in code reviews
- ✅ Accept constructive criticism gracefully
- ✅ Help others learn and grow

### For Maintainers

- ✅ Lead by example
- ✅ Be responsive to community needs
- ✅ Make decisions transparently
- ✅ Mentor new contributors
- ✅ Maintain a welcoming environment
- ✅ Support educational goals

### For Users

- ✅ Be respectful when reporting issues
- ✅ Provide clear bug reports
- ✅ Follow documentation guidelines
- ✅ Contribute positively to discussions
- ✅ Use configurations responsibly
